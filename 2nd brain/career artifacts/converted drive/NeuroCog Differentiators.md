__Work\-In\-Progress \(W\.I\.P\.\)__

__NeuroCog — What Makes Us Different__

*Our differentiators, stated as our own affirmative case*

Cam Morreale / team draft  ·  May 2026  ·  Internal reference

This is our internal articulation of what NeuroCog *is* and why it is built the way it is — the affirmative case, in our own terms, not a comparison against anyone else\. Every claim here is one we can stand behind and defend\. Use it to keep the team aligned on the story and to anchor pitch, demo, and pilot messaging\.

# 1\.  Asset\-based, strengths\-first profiling

NeuroCog starts from what a learner brings, not what they lack\. We surface strengths, skills, prior experience, languages, and non\-traditional knowledge — then build the learning path around those assets\. This is a deliberate philosophical stance \(ABCD asset mapping\), and it shapes every downstream recommendation\.

- Profiles five cognitive domains: Attention / Working Memory, Executive Functions, Memory, Visuospatial Abilities, and Language\.
- Frames the learner around capability and potential, which is especially motivating for adults re\-entering education after a setback or career break\.
- Produces a profile a learner actually wants to read about themselves — an engagement asset in its own right\.

# 2\.  Low\-friction, multimodal discovery intake

Our intake meets learners where they are\. Instead of one long form, NeuroCog triangulates signal from multiple natural modes — survey responses, video notes, handwriting upload, audio chat, and optional micro\-assessments — to understand how someone actually thinks and communicates\.

- Lower barrier to completion, which matters most for ADHD and neurodivergent learners and for busy career transitioners\.
- Captures authentic, open\-ended signal \(how someone writes, speaks, and reasons\) rather than only forced\-choice answers\.
- Confidence\-gated: the system knows how sure it is about a profile and can ask for more signal before committing to a recommendation\.

# 3\.  A named motivational architecture

Motivation is a first\-class layer in NeuroCog, grounded in established theory rather than ad\-hoc nudging\. We combine CUTRICE \(a neuro\-prism framework grounded in Self\-Determination Theory\) with Ikigai for career alignment, so recommendations connect to purpose, identity, and real\-world goals — not just skill gaps\.

- CUTRICE captures career goals, constraints, transition context, relevance to lived experience, intrinsic motivation, confidence beliefs, and economic outcomes\.
- Directly targets the core reason adult learners disengage from programs: a path that feels disconnected from why they started\.
- Because it is theory\-grounded, it gives us a principled way to explain and tune recommendations — and a clear hypothesis to validate in the pilot\.

# 4\.  Explainable, three\-path recommendations

NeuroCog does not hand the learner a black\-box answer\. For each learner it generates three distinct, explainable learning paths, each with a confidence score and plain\-language rationale the learner and instructor can both understand\.

- Strength\-Leveraging, Growth\-Stretching, and Interest\-Aligned paths — giving the learner agency to choose rather than a single take\-it\-or\-leave\-it route\.
- Every recommendation comes with a “why,” which builds trust with learners and gives advisors something to act on\.
- Explainability is a relationship feature: institutions need to defend placement decisions, and learners need to believe in their path\.

# 5\.  Built to fit the LMS, not replace it

NeuroCog is an intelligence layer that plugs into the systems institutions already run\. We deploy as an LTI 1\.3 tool, a Moodle\-native plugin, or a headless FastAPI backend — so adoption does not require ripping out an existing LMS\.

- Removes the single biggest institutional objection: “we just bought / built our LMS\.”
- Multiple integration paths let us meet an institution wherever it already is, with Moodle\-first orgs as a natural early beachhead\.
- Positions us as additive infrastructure \(placement, pathing, advising insight\) rather than a competing platform\.

# 6\.  Purpose\-built for career transitioners

NeuroCog is not a general\-purpose tutor retrofitted for adults\. The whole system — profile schema, course tagging, success metrics — is designed around people changing careers, reskilling, or re\-entering the workforce\. That focus sharpens everything from messaging to outcome definition\.

- Success is defined in career\-outcome terms \(role readiness, placement, confidence\), not generic course completion\.
- A tight ideal customer profile makes the value proposition concrete for workforce programs, bootcamps, and university advising / continuing\-education departments\.
- Clear ROI story: a scalable learner\-intelligence layer at a small fraction of what institutions already spend per student on advising and student services\.

# 7\.  Designed to adapt as it learns

NeuroCog’s architecture is built so that profiles and recommendations sharpen over time from real behavioral signal — journals, artifacts, and interaction data — rather than treating intake as a one\-time snapshot\. Demonstrating this adaptive lift is a core objective of the pilot\.

- Recommendation engine designed around online adaptation \(contextual bandits with Thompson Sampling\) to balance trying new content against reinforcing what works\.
- Profile evolution uses informal, low\-burden signals over time — no repeated formal testing required\.
- A continuous feedback loop is the long\-term moat: the more a learner uses it, the better it fits them\.
