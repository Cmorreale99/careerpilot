We are rapidly approaching the tipping point of climate change, or if the global temperature goes above a certain threshold, the effects of human induced climate change will be irreversible\. According to recent studies, if we cut our carbon emissions in half by 2030 we will have a 50% chance of keeping the global temperature under this threshold\. In 2005 our average quarterly carbon emissions was 384\.04225, and declined to 356\.549 in 2020\. Applying a linear regression formula, we would expect the average quarterly carbon emissions to decline to 339\.365719 by 2030, which would be only a 4\.81933% decrease in carbon emissions from 2020 to 2030\. To even have a 50% chance of slowing climate change down enough to avoid the tipping point, we need to start reducing our carbon emissions by more than a 10x factor\.
