Stock Market Simulation - IQP DZT- 2103
The goal of this project is to learn stock market trading strategies and get experiences by simulation. A seven-week stock market simulation was conducted using the simulation
engine Investopedia. One member used Value and Recommendation trading for Simulation 1 and Crypto trading for Simulation 4. Another member used Position trading for
Simulation 2 and Speculative trading for Simulation 3. Trading decisions were made using both fundamental analysis as well as deeper analysis based on each strategy that led to
the results of +10.98% for Simulation 1, +9.20% for Simulation 2, +24.73% for Simulation 3 and finally +8.82% for Simulation 4. Performances from all the four simulations
were better than that from the overall market which by the end of the simulation was only up 1.36%.

CDR HUA - Completion of Degree Requirement HUA 2024 Spring Semester Grade:A
Practicum: Advanced Science Writing
For this humanities practicum in advanced science writing, I examined the profitability of algorithmic cryptocurrency day-trading bots. Specifically, I conducted a meta-analysis
of eight studies exploring 7 different AI/ML algorithms. Results showed that the top algorithms are reinforcement learning (RL) and artificial neural networks (ANN). The top
predictors for price prediction were volatility and the previous day's price movement. The RL approach beat the buy and hold by roughly 400% over a 66-month period, and ANN
outperformed the buy and hold strategy by over 260% during a 30-day live trading period. Therefore, these results directly challenge the efficient market hypothesis, or the idea
that excess profits cannot be made due to all known information being priced in.
